# Pen &amp; Paper — Design Context

Single source of truth for design decisions. Every implementation skill must read this before writing code. Update when the brief changes; do not let code drift from it.

---

## Users

**Primary**: Beginners walking the author's same path. Curious people who have always wondered why anyone would pay $200, $500, $1,000 for a pen. They arrive uncertain. They stay for the story.

**Secondary**: Lurkers at any stage of the craft — from "I just received my first Lamy Safari" to "I'm deciding between a Nakaya and a Montblanc 149." Not beginners, but not looking for Reddit-grade chatter either. They come for taste and for pairings they hadn't considered.

**State of mind**: Quiet. Tactile. Seeking something hand-made among the infinite feed. The user is not rushed. They are the kind of person who closes 30 tabs to open one magazine. If the site feels like a feed, we've failed.

**Job to be done**: _"Help me understand — and eventually acquire — the pen and the paper that match who I'm becoming."_

## Brand Personality

Three words from the author, verbatim:

- **High end**
- **Exquisite**
- **Minimal**

Additional vocabulary for this brand:

- Catalogued, not curated
- Archival, not editorial-lite
- Savant, not master
- Specimen, not product
- Restraint, not reduction
- Material, not decorative

The brand voice is quiet and specific. It does not sell. It shows. It earns the reader's patience by being worth it.

## Aesthetic Direction

### References

- **Aesop** (aesop.com) — product detail pages. Typographic restraint, generous whitespace, warm neutrals, single accent color used as a whisper. The confidence of not trying.
- **M+ Museum** (mplus.org.hk) — archival presentation, monographic weight, rule lines, catalog-object reverence.
- **Highest-craft magazine editorial** — specifically the Magazine B / Apartamento / Cereal / Openhouse territory. Monograph discipline. Single-image spreads. Tracked small caps as labels. Typography that earns its page.

### Anti-references (do not build)

- Craft-blog warmth (Austin Kleon, The Pen Addict, Goulet Pens). Right subject, wrong register.
- Pinterest craft aesthetic. Wholesome is the enemy here.
- JetPens / Goulet Pens / Anderson Pens — catalog commerce. That is _not_ what we are.
- Medium / Ghost / standard blog chrome. This is not a blog.
- Tools & Toys / The Sweet Setup — affiliate-commerce polish. Right restraint, wrong soul.
- Linear / Vercel / Notion startup minimal. Cold. Tech-company-minimal is not luxury-craft-minimal.
- Kinfolk-lite lifestyle beige. Too mood-boardy, not archival enough.
- Anything that looks like "I asked an AI to make a website."

### Theme

**Light.** Warm bone ground. Ink text. The content is read in daylight or a reading lamp. Dark mode is not a priority for v1.

### Palette (OKLCH, tinted; subject to refinement in first build)

| Role                 | Token           | Value                                               |
| -------------------- | --------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| Ground               | `--ink-page`    | `oklch(96% 0.010 80)` — warm bone, ~#f4f1ea         |
| Body ink             | `--ink-body`    | `oklch(22% 0.010 70)` — warm tinted black, ~#1d1912 |
| Secondary ink        | `--ink-whisper` | `oklch(42% 0.015 70)` — warm stone, ~#635a4d        |
| Rule / hairline      | `--ink-rule`    | `oklch(82% 0.010 75)` — fine bone line              |
| Accent (one, sparse) | `--ink-accent`  | `oklch(40% 0.08 30)` — oxidized copper / ink-oak    |

The accent appears once per view at most. It is the sealing wax, not the page.

Neutrals are **tinted toward warm bone**, never pure gray. No pure white. No pure black. Luxury-craft minimal never uses either.

### Typography (subject to spec-gate; not final until approved)

All candidates chosen to avoid the banned reflex list (Fraunces, Cormorant, Playfair, Inter, DM Sans, Plus Jakarta, Instrument, Outfit, etc.).

**Display (specimen titling, large)**
Candidate: **Editorial New** (Pangram Pangram / Fontshare, free). Italic for specimen numbers. Roman for essay titles.
Alt: **Migra** (Fontshare) for more drama, **Bluu Next** for more severity.

**Text (body, pairing notes)**
Candidate: **Supreme** (Fontshare, free) — neutral grotesque, Söhne-adjacent, invisible.
Alt: **Author** (Fontshare) if we want a text serif.

**Catalog labels / metadata**
Candidate: **Supreme** at 10.5px, uppercase, tracked +0.18em. Feels like a museum tombstone label.
Alt: **Gambarino** (Fontshare) for labels with more character.

### Motion

Restrained. No bounce, no spring, no decorative parallax. Use:

- 300–500ms ease-out-quart on state changes
- Staggered page-load reveals for hero sequence (type fade-in, rule line draw)
- No hover tricks, no scroll-driven shaders
- Reduced-motion: kill all transforms, keep opacity only

### Layout

- **Asymmetric editorial grid.** 12-col base, but spreads break the grid intentionally.
- **Page-like spreads**, not dashboard-like rows. One image per spread when possible.
- **Rule lines** as the dominant structural device (hairlines, 1px, bone).
- **Generous margins.** Fluid margin clamp to keep line-length at 55–65ch.
- **Mobile**: single-column verticality. Treat as a turning catalog, not a shrunk desktop.
- **No cards.** No card shadows, no card grids. Each entry is an editorial layout, not a card.

## Design Principles

Five commitments that resolve any design disagreement.

1. **Story first, engine second.** The reader falls in love with a pen on page one. The search filter appears only after they've already been seduced. Utility is reached _through_ editorial, never around it.
2. **Cataloguing, not posting.** Every entry is a specimen in an archive. Numbered. Dated. Made to be returned to. Nothing feels disposable.
3. **Restraint is the accent.** Whitespace is a feature. One accent color, used once per view. One display font, used sparingly. Nothing decorative earns its place unless it clarifies.
4. **Material over metaphor.** Show actual pens, actual ink on actual paper. Photograph, scan, detail. Never illustrate a pen when you can show one.
5. **Earn the reader's patience.** Slow down the landing page. Let the hero breathe. Let the reader be curious. Do not chase them with CTAs.

## Information Architecture (Phase 1)

Four surfaces, in priority order:

### Surface 1 — The Catalogue (primary)

The journey, staged and chronological. Each entry is an essay with a pairing block attached.

- **Stages**: _First Pen_ → _Curious_ → _Collector_ → _Savant_ (names TBD)
- **Chronology**: entries numbered in acquisition order, date-stamped
- **Entry anatomy**: specimen label (pen · paper · ink · nib · date) + essay + photography + pairing block

### Surface 2 — The Pairing Engine (secondary)

Reached from within entries ("see other pairings for this pen," "what paper suits this nib"). Feels like a reading-room index, not a search box. Tracked small caps. Rule lines. No "filter chips."

### Surface 3 — The Register (tertiary)

Catalog browser: pens and papers, sortable by attributes — price tier, weight, nib fineness, fill mechanism, paper weight (gsm), paper finish. Still looks like an archive. Still typographic.

### Surface 4 — Acquisition (v1 light-touch)

Per-entry affiliate links or shop recommendations. Not a store. No cart.

(Phase 2, not in this brief: community, local shops, user submissions, accounts.)

## Content Model

The atomic unit is the **Specimen Entry**:

```
Specimen №    — e.g. Specimen 007
Stage         — First Pen | Curious | Collector | Savant
Acquired      — YYYY-MM-DD
Pen           — make, model, nib, fill mechanism, weight
Paper         — mill, line, gsm, finish
Ink           — maker, color, saturation notes
Essay         — art + science + personality + personal
Photography   — ink-on-paper samples + pen detail
Pairing note  — why these three belong together
```

Non-pairing essays (e.g. "The history of Tomoe River," "Nib flex for the curious") exist as **Field Notes** — separate content type, linked to Specimen Entries where relevant.

## Constraints

- **Stack**: TypeScript strict. **Next.js 15 App Router** + **Payload CMS 3** (installed inside the Next.js app). Postgres via Neon. Admin UI at `/admin`. Editorial pages statically rendered with on-demand revalidation on CMS change.
- **No Astro.** Author has prior bad experience; not to be reintroduced.
- **Content + catalog in CMS.** Specimens, Pens, Papers, Pairings, Field Notes all live as Payload collections. Rich text via Lexical (not MDX).
- **Hosting**: Vercel. Domain TBD.
- **Performance budget**: Lighthouse 95+ across all categories on editorial pages. First contentful paint under 1.2s. Zero client JS on editorial pages (client components restricted to `/register` filters and `/quiz` flow).
- **Accessibility**: WCAG AA minimum, AAA for body text contrast. 4.5:1 body. Prefers-reduced-motion respected. Keyboard-navigable. Alt text on every photograph.
- **Image pipeline**: Hand-authored photography + Leonardo AI where appropriate. Vercel Blob storage. AVIF / WebP via `next/image` + Payload's upload plugin.
- **Pairing Quiz**: client-rendered against a pre-built `catalog.json` index. 6 questions, top-3 result. Lives at `/quiz`.

## Anti-Goals

What getting this wrong looks like:

- **It looks like every other blog.** If someone says "reminds me of Medium," delete it.
- **It looks like a store.** Anderson Pens, JetPens, Goulet — if there's any hint of commerce-first framing, rebuild the page.
- **It looks designed by an AI.** Gradient text. Side-stripe cards. Glowing accents on dark. Generic card grids. Stock "reading" illustrations. All bans in `/impeccable` apply.
- **It looks craft-precious.** Pen sites often over-rotate on wood-grain backgrounds, ornamental dividers, handwriting fonts. We are luxury-craft, not rustic-craft.
- **It looks like a startup.** Inter, DM Sans, Satoshi-as-everything, violet accent, glass cards. All banned.
- **The engine dominates the story.** If a first-time visitor lands on a search page rather than a specimen spread, we inverted the site.

## Open Questions (for the implementer, not blocking)

- Exact stage names — "First Pen / Curious / Collector / Savant" is a working spine; may get sharper. Possible alternatives: "Entry / Appetite / Collection / Mastery" or Roman-numeral tiers (I–IV) with archival captions.
- Domain and site name. Candidates: penandpaper.co, specimen.co, the-register.co, etc. Decide before first build.
- Photography pipeline — stock vs. hand-photographed vs. Leonardo-generated for early entries before the author has acquired the pens.
- Whether Field Notes surface on the home page or only through cross-links.
